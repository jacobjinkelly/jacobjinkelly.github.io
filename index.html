I study Computer Science, Mathematics, and Statistics at the University of Toronto. I'm currently working with David Duvenaud at the Vector Institute on improving the efficiency of neural ODEs. Previously, I worked with Benjamin Haibe-Kains at Princess Margaret Cancer Research on benchmarking machine learning techniques for inferring sample-specific transcriptional networks from single-cell RNA-seq data, and predicting drug response of cell lines from gene expression for patients with acute myeloid leukemia.

My long-term goal is to improve healthcare through technology. Towards this goal, I'm interested in researching how machine learning combined with novel sources of data can be used to develop new tools for improved diagnosis and treatment of patients. I'm particularly interested in latent variable models, neural ODEs, variational inference, and genomics.

Some of my hobbies include running, rock climbing, and reading.
